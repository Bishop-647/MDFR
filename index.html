About Us

Built Different.
Built Real.

Most simulation servers call themselves realistic. We built our department from the ground up to actually operate like one. Every rank, every SOP, every protocol mirrors the structure of the real Miami-Dade Fire Rescue.

Founded by experienced players with real-world firefighting knowledge, our server is led by Chief Roscoe — an active firefighter in the UK — and Deputy Chief on the administrative side. We bring credibility others can't match.

Whether you're a serious sim enthusiast or someone looking to learn what the fire service is really about — this is where you belong.

Standard Operating Procedures

How We Operate

🔊
Radio Discipline

All communications follow proper radio etiquette. Units identify by designation, use 10-codes where applicable, and maintain clear brevity on all channels.

🚨
Incident Command

Every active incident establishes a clear IC. Scene size-up, resource requests, and tactical assignments follow NIMS ICS structure at all times.

🚒
Apparatus Response

Unit response follows dispatch priority levels. All apparatus operates under departmental response zones with proper staging and positioning procedures.

👔
Conduct & Professionalism

Members represent MDFR at all times. Professional conduct is mandatory both on scene and in department communications. Zero tolerance for misconduct.

📝
Reporting & Documentation

Significant incidents require after-action documentation. Members log training hours and notable calls in accordance with departmental record keeping.

🎓
Training Requirements

All members must complete department orientation before active deployment. Ongoing training is required to maintain rank and eligibility for promotion.
